Partners in Education
An online community for menopausal issues

Therese Hendricks, Anthony Lim, Todd Sjoblom and Shyam Visweswaran
Mission Statement
  • Provide patient and physician education on menopause in order to increase the effectiveness of patient/physician communications.

  • Provide an online community of patients and physicians supporting their patients in managing the symptoms of menopause.
Do you need help in understanding the common symptoms and latest therapies on menopause?
If you are a woman approaching or undergoing menopause, generally 40 years of age or greater, and are concerned about:
  • what can I anticipate (common symptoms)

  • how can I maximize my likelihood of a healthy transition into menopause

  • what are my risks for heart disease and breast cancer during this time

  • what therapies are recommended to manage the symptoms and control the risks

then this program is for you. By forming patient and support groups, through initial face-to-face meetings and an online community, you will come to understand and maximize your sense of well-being and likelihood of a smooth transition.

How will my physician be involved?
You and your physician will be enrolled in the Partners In Education (PIE) program on menopause. PIE is a non-profit organization of educators dedicated to improving patient and physician understanding of the common symptoms, increased risks of disease (eg, heart and breast cancer), and range of therapies for managing menopause.

There are 3 parts to the program:

  1. You (the patient) will first attend a 50-minute group meeting with similar patients (from your physician's practice) in which:

    • A PIE physician/leader, a specialist in the area of menopause, will present a program introduction and overview of symptoms and therapies of menopause

    • You will receive an initial assessment of your individual risks of heart disease and breast cancer

    • You will participate in a group discussion with your community of patients and physician/leader, to hear about common concerns and answer your questions

  2. You will have an individual office visit with your physician, in which the foundation for a meaningful two-way discussion has been established based on your prior group meeting and initial risk assessments, and based on your physician having similarly undergone a review of the program and latest studies/therapies on menopause.

  3. You will receive ongoing support, via the online community, in which you have immediate access to:

    • Messages from your physician tailored to your symptoms

    • A question and answer (Q&A) forum

    • Selected publications recommended by your physician

What does the physician gain?
A primary care physician, otherwise facing insufficient time to review the latest studies/therapies on menopause, and insufficient time to meet with, discuss and track his/her patients, will be provided with:

  • A better informed patient and preliminary diagnostic assessments at the initial patient office visit, greatly enhancing the quality of the visit and resulting satisfaction of both the patient and physician

  • Immediate access to the latest studies and treatments on menopause, including a discussion forum among peer physicians

  • A quick and easy-to-use way to communicate to groups of his/her patients recommended publications, treatment schedules, advice on symptoms and side effects to watch for, office visit reminders, etc.
